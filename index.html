Bensonhurst and COVID-19 Numbers

October 7th, 2021

The streets of Bensonhurst are unusually empty, due to COVID-19 concerns

COVID-19 In Bensonhurst

The neighborhood of Bensonhurst is one of the areas of south Brooklyn that has been hit the hardest from the Covid-19 pandemic. One in seven people have been diagnosed with the virus, and since the start of the pandemic there has been about 4,330 deaths. Over the course of the pandemic, community centres and businesses have faced closures, as well as changing restrictions. The once lively and bustling neighborhood has quieted down due to fears of reinfection and transmission.

The data on the website conveys the current COVID-19 numbers in Bensonhurst. The icons below (from left to right) represent the total population, total cases, deaths, and the total population of people who have been vaccinated. All of this data has been pulled from the NYC health government page, and has been a key part of the articles I've written about the area.

Population

The total population of Bensonhurst is 77,087 people.

Reported Cases

The total number of reported COVID-19 cases in Bensonhurst is 10,180 cases. This means that one in seven people in Bensonhurst have been diagnosed with COVID-19.

Deaths

The total number of COVID-19 deaths in Bensonhurst are 272 deaths. This means that one in every 283 people in this neighborhood have died from COVID-19.

Vaccine Rate

The total number of vaccinated individuals in Bensonhurst is 46,701. These people have all received at least their first dose. Of this number, 42,704 people have received both their first and second dose and are considered fully vaccinated.
